Reading Course on Category Theory

Introduction

When studying mathematics you will (or already have) recognize certain patterns.
You consider sets with nice properties and morphisms between them, that respect your properties.
For example: vector spaces and linear maps between them, or topological spaces and continuous maps.
Such a pair of interesting objects and structure-preserving morphisms is called a category.
Categories are a fundamental building block in Mathematics.
Therefore by understanding Category Theory one also gains a deeper understanding of Mathematics in general.
In this reading course we will introduce the basic concepts of Category Theory such as universal properties, functors, natural transformations, limits, representability and the Yoneda Lemma.
